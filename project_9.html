1. Project Overview

This project focused on ensuring the operational integrity of critical telecommunications infrastructure through targeted hardware maintenance and diagnostics. The scope involved reactive repairs to enterprise servers (Oracle and Dell) to restore redundancy and performance, as well as complex troubleshooting of backup storage peripherals (Tape Drives) to ensure data protection capabilities were maintained.

2. Technical Scope

Server Hardware Maintenance: Component-level replacements (RAID Cards, HDDs, Power Supplies) on Oracle and Dell enterprise servers.
Backup Infrastructure: Diagnostic extraction and connectivity troubleshooting for HPE Tape Drives used in NetBackup environments.
Power Redundancy: Restoring power resilience in high-performance servers through mass power supply unit (PSU) replacements.

3. My Contributions

Phase 1: Oracle Server Maintenance

  • RAID Card Replacement: Replaced a faulty RAID card in an Oracle server at SLT Welikada DC.
  • Technology Application: Applied RAID technology knowledge to ensure the server's storage redundancy was correctly restored.

Phase 2: Tape Drive Troubleshooting

  • Drive Dump Retrieval: Retrieved drive logs from HPE tape drives at SLT main branch.
  • Connectivity Troubleshooting: Configured direct laptop connection with matching IPv4 range to establish communication.
  • Diagnostic Software: Used HPE Library and Tape Tools (LTT) to download drive dumps and resolved version incompatibility issues.

Phase 3: Dell Server & Power Maintenance

  • HDD Replacement: Replaced failed HDD in a Dell Server at SLT IDC.
  • Mass Power Supply Replacement: Replaced 12 power supplies on an SLT M6 server over two days to ensure full power redundancy.
